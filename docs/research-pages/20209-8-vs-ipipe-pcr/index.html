Fall20-W8 IPIPE PCR

Objective#

This object of this lab session is to amplify the gene insert fragment of the recombinant plasmid FtFBA-pNIC-BSa4 as the second segment of the PIPE process.

Methods (modified from week 2)#

  1. Primer Dilution for forward and reverse primers.

    1. Dilute forward and reverse primer to stock solution.

      The primers were diluted to 100 Î¼M stock solution with Tris.

    2. Dilute stock solutions into working solution.

The primers were diluted to <Measure unit="um">10</Measure> working solution.
Amount of working solution required for each primer:
3 replicas * <Measure unit="ul">1.25</Measure> = <Measure unit="ul">3.75</Measure>
Thus, <Measure unit="ul">5</Measure> of working solution was prepared by diluting <Measure unit="ul">.5</Measure> stock solution to <Measure unit="ul">5</Measure> with ddH2O.
  1. I-PIPE PCR
I-PIPE PCR was used to create the gene insert for the recombinant PCR.
Prepared and ran V-PIPE cycle for 2 <Measure unit="ul">25</Measure> samples.
See [Lab Note Protocol](/docs/lab-notes/wetlab/pcr/pipe-cloning#v-pipe-cycle) for detail.

  1. Verification

    A gel was ran on the I-PIPE product to detect the presence of expected product (~ 1100 bp).

    Fig 1. Gel image for I-PIPE product. S1=Sample 1; S2=Sample 2; NA=Not Applicable; Ldr=NEB 100bp ladder

Discussion#

Generally the results look good. The gel look very similar to the V-PIPE lab in that while it has a clear band at the expected length, there seems to be another band that is at around 100~200 bp region. I can think of two hypothesis for this:

(1) It may be still due to secondary structure on DNA, but such a large distance between the extra band from the main band makes this not very likely the case.

(2) Since we have multiple primer secondary structures (self-dimers, hetero-dimers) that can exist, and the PIPE primers are long (forward is 32bp and reverse is 51bp), they may be able to generate a band that looks like ~ 100bp DNA.

Next Steps#

  • Sequencing Verification on PCR product.
  • Ligation of I-PIPE and V-PIPE product.
